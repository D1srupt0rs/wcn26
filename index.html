FAQ

What is World Cup NFTs 2026?

World Cup NFTs 2026 is a one-time NFT event built around the FIFA World Cup 2026.

There are:

  • 48 countries
  • 200 NFTs per nation
  • 9600 NFTs total

Each NFT represents a country competing in the tournament through premium collectible artwork inspired by football culture and national identity.

How does the mint work?

The mint is completely random.

When you mint, you do NOT choose:

  • the country
  • the rarity
  • the NFT you receive

Every mint is blind.

You could pull:

  • a tournament favorite
  • an underdog nation
  • a Common
  • a Rare
  • or a Legendary NFT
Can I choose my favorite country later?

Yes.

After you mint, you can trade your NFTs on the secondary market.

This means you can:

  • buy nations you believe in
  • collect your favorite teams
  • upgrade your rarity
  • sell countries you no longer support
What are the NFT rarities?

Each country contains:

  • Common → 150 NFTs → x1 reward multiplier
  • Rare → 40 NFTs → x2 reward multiplier
  • Legendary → 10 NFTs → x5 reward multiplier
What is the mint price?

Mint price is 0.02 SOL per NFT.

How are rewards distributed?

At the end of the World Cup, the nation that wins the FINAL unlocks the reward pool.

Only holders of NFTs from the winning country receive rewards.

Reward payouts depend on NFT rarity:

  • Common → x1
  • Rare → x2
  • Legendary → x5
Where does the reward pool come from?

80% of all mint funds go directly into the reward pool.

Additionally:

  • 10% royalties apply on secondary market trades
  • 50% of those royalties are added back into the reward pool

More trading activity = bigger potential rewards.

What happens if the collection does not fully mint out?

Secondary market trading will open shortly after the mint starts. The system still works normally.

The reward pool is based on:

  • total NFTs minted
  • secondary market royalties

If fewer NFTs mint, the pool is smaller — but there are also fewer holders sharing rewards.

Because the mint is random, nobody knows:

  • which countries will become scarce
  • which nations will have the fewest holders
  • how many winning NFTs will exist
What happens to NFTs from losing countries?

NFTs from nations that do not win will not receive final rewards.

However, they still remain:

  • collectible artworks
  • tradable assets
  • part of the official World Cup NFTs 2026 collection

Their value may still change based on rarity, demand and collector interest.

Is this a long-term project?

No.

World Cup NFTs 2026 is designed as a one-time event project tied directly to the FIFA World Cup 2026.

The entire experience revolves around:

  • minting
  • trading
  • speculation
  • supporting nations
